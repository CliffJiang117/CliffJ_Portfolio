ちゅうごく현지인처럼 体验

Experimental Game / Live Experience · 2025

An experimental live game designed to create a deliberate sense of unfamiliarity. Players enter a socially ambiguous environment where they must interpret hidden rules through observation, body language, spatial behavior, and embodied interaction.

I served as the lead experience designer, establishing the core concept, rule system, and player interaction structure. I also created key visual assets, including in-game currency, and performed as an in-game character to directly shape how players understood the experience.

This project explores how game systems can exist beyond formal rules, emerging instead through perception, behavior, and social negotiation.

From Bug to Meme: How Translation Errors Became Gaming Culture

Handmade Zine / Critical Game Analysis · 2026

A handmade zine that explores how technical limitations, localization errors, and system constraints in games can evolve into shared cultural meaning among players.

The zine is physically assembled using layered layouts, cutouts, and inserted elements. Its visual design mirrors the fragmented and reconstructed feeling of encountering broken or recontextualized language in games.

Through case studies such as unofficial Pokémon translations and recurring dialogue bugs, the project examines how players reinterpret unintended outputs of game systems. Rather than treating these moments only as errors, the zine presents them as artifacts that become part of a game's cultural identity through repetition and community circulation.
