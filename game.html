Game Projects

Selected game projects, prototypes, and gameplay systems focused on player interaction, system-driven progression, procedural spaces, and experimental rules.

Take Me to the Golden Ring

Platformer · GDevelop 5 · 2024

A platformer centered around a player-driven path creation mechanic, where players draw their own routes to navigate the environment.

I led the design and implementation of the core gameplay system, as well as level design, UI, and overall integration. The core mechanic allows players to draw paths using the mouse, generating temporary platforms that enable traversal across gaps and obstacles.

Instead of relying on fixed level geometry, players actively construct their own movement routes, turning navigation into a creative problem-solving process. The level design places obstacles and collectibles in positions that require player-generated solutions.

Matrix

Interactive Narrative · Twine · 2024

A Twine-based interactive narrative centered on a time loop system, where progression is driven by player knowledge rather than traditional collection or combat mechanics.

The game places the player in a repeating cycle where death resets the world, but key knowledge persists across loops. Through repeated playthroughs, players gradually unlock new dialogue options, narrative paths, and strategic choices based on what they have learned.

The system uses variables to track time, memory, and critical discoveries, allowing story events and character interactions to change dynamically. Certain encounters are intentionally unwinnable in early loops, requiring players to experiment, fail, and accumulate understanding before they can progress.

Escape the Factory

Platformer · JavaScript / Phaser · 2025

A platformer centered on timing-based traversal, where players navigate industrial hazards and collect key items to unlock progression and hidden paths.

I developed this project using Phaser, serving as the programmer and level designer. The game emphasizes core platforming mechanics, including precise jumping, hazard avoidance, and collectible-driven progression.

I implemented an environmental system where falling debris lands on a crusher, temporarily forming platforms that are gradually destroyed. These temporary platforms function as both traversal tools and environmental hazards, creating time-sensitive movement challenges.

Shoot to Win!

Top-Down Roguelike Shooter · JavaScript / Phaser · 2025

A top-down roguelike shooter where players survive against waves of enemies in procedurally generated environments.

I served as a programmer focusing on movement systems, AI behavior, and procedural level generation. My primary contribution was implementing pathfinding for player and enemy navigation, along with a terrain-based movement system where different surfaces affect speed.

I also developed the procedural map generation system. To ensure consistent navigation and avoid disconnected areas, I structured the algorithm around a main-road network with branching paths. This project explores how procedural generation, terrain rules, and AI pathfinding can work together to create replayable combat spaces.
